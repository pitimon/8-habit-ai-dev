# EU AI Act → 8-Habit Skills Mapping Guide

**For**: Developers building high-risk AI systems for the EU market
**Purpose**: Map EU AI Act obligations (Articles 9-15) to 8-habit-ai-dev skills you can invoke today
**Enforcement date**: 2 August 2026

> ⚠️ **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** This guide is a developer reference, not legal counsel. Article references are based on Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (Official Journal version of 13 June 2024) cross-verified with EC AI Act Service Desk. The Aug 2 2026 date is firm in current text but subject to Digital Omnibus harmonized standards conditionality. **Consult a qualified EU AI lawyer before using this guide for production compliance.** See `docs/research/eu-ai-act-obligations.md` for primary-source verified quotes per article.

## How to Use This Guide

The EU AI Act doesn't tell you _how_ to comply — it tells you _what_ to prove. This guide bridges the gap by mapping each obligation to a concrete 8-habit-ai-dev skill that produces audit-ready evidence.

### 3-Step Workflow

1. **Scope check**: Run `/eu-ai-act-check --scope` first. If your AI system is **not high-risk** under Annex III or **not deployed in the EU**, you can skip this entire guide. Most internal tools, dev tooling, and non-safety AI fall outside scope.

2. **Map obligations to skills**: Use the table below to find which 8-habit skill addresses each obligation. Most obligations map to skills you already use — you just need to capture the output as evidence.

3. **Generate evidence**: Each skill produces an artifact. Save it under `docs/compliance/eu-ai-act/<obligation>/` with a date stamp. That folder becomes your audit trail.

### One-Time Bootstrap

Before your first compliance check, create the evidence folder structure (the skills will NOT auto-create it — you control what gets versioned):

```bash
mkdir -p docs/compliance/eu-ai-act/{01-risk-mgmt,02-data-gov,03-tech-docs/annex-iv,04-records,05-transparency,06-oversight,07-accuracy,08-robustness,09-cybersecurity,reports,scope-decision,ai-dev-log}
echo "# EU AI Act Evidence — Generated by 8-habit-ai-dev compliance skills" > docs/compliance/eu-ai-act/README.md
git add docs/compliance/eu-ai-act/README.md && git commit -m "chore: bootstrap EU AI Act evidence folders"
```

This creates 9 obligation folders + reports + scope-decision + ai-dev-log directories.

### What This Guide Does NOT Do

- ❌ Make legal determinations — talk to a lawyer for Annex III classification
- ❌ Replace conformity assessment (Article 43) — that's a separate process
- ❌ Cover Articles 16+ (deployer obligations, post-market reporting) — provider-side only
- ❌ Cover Article 50 (general-purpose AI transparency) — different rule set

## Detailed Mapping

### 1. Risk Management System (Article 9)

**What the law wants**: Continuous, iterative risk identification and mitigation throughout the AI system lifecycle.

**How 8-habit-ai-dev helps**:

```
/security-check    # H1 — produces risk register with mitigations
/design            # H8 — feeds risk classification into architecture
```

**Invocation example**:

```
/security-check on this PR
→ produces report with: known risks, mitigations, residual risk
→ save to: docs/compliance/eu-ai-act/01-risk-mgmt/2026-08-01-pr-123.md
```

**Why this works**: `/security-check` already covers OWASP DSGAI (per Issue #60). Article 9 just requires you to keep the output as a dated artifact.

### 4. Record-Keeping (Article 12)

**What the law wants**: Automatic event logging during system operation for risk identification and post-market monitoring.

**How 8-habit-ai-dev helps**:

```
/monitor-setup  # H7 — defines logging strategy and retention
/reflect        # H7 — periodic review of operational findings
```

**Invocation example**:

```
/monitor-setup for production deployment
```

**Note**: 8-habit-ai-dev is a discipline plugin, not a runtime telemetry system. `/monitor-setup` produces the **strategy**; you implement the actual logging in your application code.

### 6. Human Oversight (Article 14) ⭐ Three Loops Integration

**What the law wants**: Effective human oversight — design enables shutdown, override, and prevents over-reliance.

**How 8-habit-ai-dev helps**:

```
/design         # H8 — Three Loops decision model classifies each decision
/review-ai      # H4 — human review gate before merging AI-generated code
```

**Invocation example**:

```
/design "AI-assisted lending decision system"
→ output includes Three Loops Classification table:
   - Loan approval >$X → In-the-Loop (human required)
   - Loan approval <$X → On-the-Loop (AI proposes, human confirms)
   - UI rendering → Out-of-Loop (autonomous)
→ save to: docs/compliance/eu-ai-act/06-oversight/oversight-design.md
```

**🔗 Why this is the killer integration**: Article 14 mandates specific human-oversight capabilities (override, stop, intervene, disregard). The Three Loops design pattern is one way to **satisfy** those capabilities — it is NOT a term used by EU law itself. When auditors ask "show me your human oversight design," you point to your `/design` output and cite Article 14 ¶4(a-e) directly. The Three Loops table organizes _how_ you satisfy each ¶, but auditors will check your evidence against the regulation's text, not your framework labels.

> ⚠️ **Important framing**: The "Three Loops" model originates from human-autonomy teaming literature (Endsley 1999, DARPA), not EU law. Article 14 provides the legal _capabilities_ requirement; Three Loops is one valid design pattern that meets it.

### 9. Cybersecurity (Article 15 ¶5) ⭐ DSGAI Integration

**What the law wants**: Appropriate cybersecurity protecting model integrity, access, and supply chain.

**How 8-habit-ai-dev helps**:

```
/security-check  # H1 — runs OWASP DSGAI 11-control checklist
```

**Invocation example**:

```
/security-check --dsgai
→ output includes: 11 DSGAI controls pass/fail with evidence
→ save to: docs/compliance/eu-ai-act/09-cybersecurity/dsgai-audit.md
```

**🔗 Why this is the second killer integration**: Article 15 ¶5 names exactly 5 AI-specific attack categories (data poisoning, model poisoning, adversarial examples/evasion, confidentiality attacks, model flaws). OWASP DSGAI 11 controls (Issue #60) provide the _concrete operational checklist_ for each category. Article 15 ¶5 = the "WHAT to defend against"; DSGAI = the "HOW to defend".

You're building an AI-powered medical triage assistant for the EU market. Here's the full discipline flow:

```bash

# Step 1: Brainstorm + Research

# Step 2: Requirements with EARS notation (Issue #61)
/requirements

# Step 3: Design with Three Loops (Issue #59)
/design
→ Three Loops table → covers Article 14 (human oversight)
→ Data inventory → covers Article 10 (data governance)
→ System design → covers Article 11 (technical docs)

# Step 4: Breakdown + Build-Brief
/breakdown
/build-brief

# Step 5: Security review with DSGAI (Issue #60)
/security-check --dsgai
→ DSGAI 11 controls → covers Article 15 ¶5 (cybersecurity)
→ Risk register → covers Article 9 (risk management)
→ Adversarial tests → covers Article 15 ¶4 (robustness)

# Step 6: Review
/review-ai
→ Accuracy baselines → covers Article 15 ¶1-3 (accuracy)

# Step 7: Deploy + Monitor
/deploy-guide
/monitor-setup
→ Logging strategy → covers Article 12 (record-keeping)

# Step 8: Final compliance check
/eu-ai-act-check
→ 9-row report: 9/9 obligations have evidence artifacts ✅

# Generate dev log for audit trail
/ai-dev-log
→ docs/compliance/eu-ai-act/00-dev-log/2026-Q3.md
```

Result: A complete audit trail produced as a side-effect of disciplined development. No extra compliance work — the discipline _is_ the compliance.

## What 8-habit-ai-dev Does NOT Cover

Be honest about scope:

| Out of Scope                          | Why                                                |
| ------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| Annex III risk classification         | Legal judgment — talk to a lawyer                  |
| Conformity assessment (Article 43)    | Separate certification process                     |
| Notified body interaction             | External certification body required               |
| Article 50 transparency (general AI)  | Different rule set                                 |
| Articles 16-29 (deployer obligations) | We're a development discipline, not deployment ops |
| Runtime telemetry implementation      | We produce strategy, not infrastructure            |

For these, you need:

- **Legal counsel** for classification + certification
- **Runtime systems** (Datadog, OpenTelemetry, etc.) for actual logging
- **GRC tools** (OneTrust, Drata, etc.) for full compliance workflow

8-habit-ai-dev sits at the **development discipline layer** — making sure that what you build is _build-ready for compliance_. It's not the entire compliance stack.
